# For Investors

NovaDAO provides dual protection for investors: mechanistic onchain controls enforced by Soroban smart contracts, and legal frameworks that give token holders real recourse if teams act in bad faith.

This is not a trust-based system. It is a verification-based system built on the Stellar blockchain, where every treasury operation, every governance decision, and every token issuance is permanently recorded and independently auditable.

---

## Mechanistic Protection Against Treasury Rugs

The single largest risk in token investing is the treasury rug: a founding team raises millions through a token sale, then misappropriates the funds while token holders have no recourse.

This is not a theoretical concern. The history of crypto fundraising is littered with examples of projects that raised enormous sums and then either directly misappropriated funds or allowed them to be spent without accountability.

The common pattern is straightforward: funds go to a wallet controlled by the team, the team spends the funds however they see fit, and token holders have no mechanism to intervene or recover capital.

### How NovaDAO Prevents This

On NovaDAO, **all ICO funds go directly to a market-governed treasury** implemented as a Soroban smart contract on Stellar. The founding team does not control the treasury. Nobody controls the treasury -- it is governed by decision markets.

Here is what this means in practice:

1. **Every expenditure requires a governance proposal.** Teams receive a monthly operating budget (capped at 1/6 of the minimum raise). Anything beyond that requires a public proposal that passes through decision markets.

2. **Token holders can propose capital returns.** If a team is mismanaging funds, any stakeholder can create a proposal to return treasury capital to token holders. If the market agrees that returning capital increases token value, the proposal passes automatically.

3. **All treasury flows are onchain.** Every XLM and USDC movement from the treasury is recorded on the Stellar ledger and verifiable through Stellar Expert, StellarChain, or any Horizon API endpoint. There is no offchain spending, no corporate bank account, no opacity.

<Note>
The bid wall mechanism provides an additional safety net: during the first 90 days after an ICO, token holders can sell their tokens back at Net Asset Value (NAV). This creates a price floor that protects against immediate post-ICO collapse. See the [Bid Wall](/how-launches-work/bid-wall) documentation for details.
</Note>

## Legal Protection Against Revenue Rugs

Mechanistic protections handle treasury funds. But there is a second attack vector: the revenue rug. This occurs when a project generates significant protocol revenue but routes it to a corporate entity rather than to token holders.

In traditional DeFi, this is extremely common. A protocol generates millions in fees, but those fees flow to a private company (the "Labs" entity) while token holders -- who bear all the governance and market risk -- receive nothing.

### How NovaDAO Prevents This

NovaDAO-launched projects operate through legal entities that bind protocol revenue to the governance-controlled treasury. This means:

- **Revenue flows to the treasury, not to a private company.** The legal structure ensures that fees generated by the protocol are directed to the onchain treasury governed by token holders.

- **Token holders have legal recourse.** If a service provider attempts to withhold revenue, token holders can pursue litigation through the entity structure. This is not a handshake agreement -- it is a legal obligation.

- **IP is transferred to the project entity.** Domains, code repositories, social media accounts, and other intellectual property are transferred during the listing process. The team operates as a service provider to the DAO, not as the owner of the underlying assets.

<Warning>
Legal protections are only as strong as the jurisdictional framework and entity structure. NovaDAO works with legal counsel to establish appropriate entity structures, but investors should always conduct their own due diligence and consult with legal advisors in their jurisdictions.
</Warning>

## How Decision Markets Protect You

Traditional token governance uses one-token-one-vote systems. These are trivially captured by insiders who accumulate governance tokens and vote for self-serving proposals.

NovaDAO uses **decision markets** instead of voting. Here is why this is better for investors:

### Governance Capture Is Structurally Difficult

In a voting system, an attacker needs to accumulate enough tokens to outvote everyone else. In a decision market, an attacker needs to move the market price in a direction that is economically irrational. The entire market is betting against them. If someone tries to pass a proposal that destroys value, traders will short the pass market and long the fail market -- and they will profit from doing so.

### Information Is Aggregated Efficiently

Prediction markets consistently outperform other decision-making mechanisms because they aggregate information from all participants, weighted by conviction. Traders who have better information make larger bets. Traders who are wrong lose money. The result is a governance system that finds the right answer faster and more reliably than any committee or vote.

### No Voter Apathy

In token-voting systems, participation rates are typically below 10%. Governance is decided by a tiny minority. In decision markets, participation is incentivized -- you can profit by trading correctly. This means more participants, better information, and more robust outcomes.
