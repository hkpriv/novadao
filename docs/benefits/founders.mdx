# For Entrepreneurs

NovaDAO is a place for crypto-native enterprises to raise money that does not suck.

If you are building something real and you want to fund it through a token, you have options. But most of those options are bad. We built NovaDAO because founders deserve a capital-raising platform that aligns with their long-term interests -- not one that turns them into exit liquidity machines.

Let us walk through the landscape honestly.

---

## The Five Ways to Raise Capital in Crypto

### Option 1: The Standard Playbook

The dominant model in crypto fundraising follows a well-known script:

1. Raise a seed round from VCs at a low valuation
2. Raise a Series A at a higher valuation
3. Announce a token with complex tokenomics and a multi-year unlock schedule
4. List on centralized exchanges with heavy marketing
5. Run airdrops to generate initial demand
6. Watch the token slowly bleed as insider unlocks create sustained selling pressure

This model optimizes for one thing: extracting capital from retail investors during the initial demand window. Founders who follow this path often find themselves trapped -- beholden to VCs, unable to pivot their tokenomics, and presiding over a demoralized community that bought at the top.

<Warning>
The standard playbook creates an adversarial relationship between founders and their community. When your insiders are unlocking into a declining market, you are not building together -- you are exiting onto each other.
</Warning>

### Option 2: Never Launch a Token

This is a perfectly reasonable approach. Build a company, raise venture capital or equity, and operate as a traditional business. Many successful crypto projects have taken this path.

The downside is that you miss the most powerful growth mechanism available in crypto: community economic ownership. When your users have no financial stake in your success, they are customers. When they own tokens with real governance rights and economic value, they become evangelists, contributors, and long-term partners.

### Option 3: Bonding Curve Platforms

Platforms like pump.fun and similar bonding curve launchers have gained popularity for their simplicity. But the numbers tell the real story:

- Median total fees generated per token: between $10K and $100K
- Token holders receive no governance rights
- No treasury, no IP transfer, no legal structure
- Automated snipers routinely accumulate 20-40% of supply in the first seconds
- Tokens have no fundamental value -- pure speculation

If your project is serious, a bonding curve launch signals that it is not.

### Option 4: A Standard ICO

Running your own ICO is possible. Platforms exist to facilitate this on various blockchains. But this approach requires:

- Significant trust from investors (no mechanistic protections)
- Extensive legal infrastructure you must build yourself
- Your own smart contract audits and deployment
- Manual treasury management

A standard ICO can work, but it places the entire burden of trust, compliance, and execution on the founding team.

## What Makes NovaDAO Different on Stellar

Building on the Stellar blockchain gives NovaDAO structural advantages that are not available on other chains:

### Near-Zero Participation Costs

On Stellar, every transaction costs a fraction of a cent. This means that governance participation, ICO contributions, and token trading are accessible to everyone -- not just whales who can afford gas wars. When a proposal goes to market, thousands of participants can trade without fee friction distorting the outcome.

### 5-Second Finality

When an investor commits USDC to your ICO, it settles in 5 seconds. When a governance vote resolves, the result is final in 5 seconds. There is no mempool manipulation, no MEV extraction, no frontrunning. The Stellar Consensus Protocol provides deterministic finality that makes the entire launch and governance process cleaner and more trustworthy.

### Native DEX Integration

Stellar has a built-in decentralized exchange (SDEX) at the protocol level. NovaDAO's decision markets operate directly on SDEX, which means:

- No dependency on third-party DEX protocols that could be exploited or shut down
- Orderbook-based trading with transparent price discovery
- Cross-asset liquidity that is native to the chain, not fragmented across AMM pools

### Soroban Smart Contracts

NovaDAO's governance, treasury management, bid wall, and performance package systems are implemented as Soroban smart contracts. Soroban uses Rust compiled to WASM, providing:

- Predictable execution costs
- Strong type safety and memory safety
- Formal verification capabilities
- Auditable, deterministic execution
